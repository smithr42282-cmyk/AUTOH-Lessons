Customer Concern: Vehicle Pulls to the Right After Suspension Repair

Diagnostic Procedure  ·  Check & Advise  ·  No Test Drive Required

📋 Your Assignment

Read the repair order below. Work through the full diagnostic procedure without a test drive — static inspection and alignment machine data only. Reference the related Canvas pages listed in each section. At the bottom, explain your recommended fix and complete the knowledge check.

Canvas References to review before starting: Alignment Angles Reference  ·  Vehicle-Specific Alignment Procedures  ·  Suspension Component Inspection Notes

🔧 Repair Order — Vehicle Information

Vehicle

Year / Make / Model: 2019 Honda Accord Sport

Engine: 1.5T CVT  |  Mileage: 58,420 mi

Drive Type: FWD

Previous Repair (same visit)

Right front strut assembly replaced — OEM Honda strut, spring, and upper mount

No alignment performed after strut replacement. Customer was told to return for alignment; returned same day with concern.

Customer Concern

"After getting my strut replaced, the car pulls hard to the right. It didn't pull before the repair. It pulls constantly — I have to hold the steering wheel to the left to drive straight."

Step 1 — Verify the Concern (Static, No Test Drive)

What to Check — Starting the Vehicle

Start the engine and observe with the vehicle in park on a flat, level surface:

  1.  With hands off the wheel, observe if the steering wheel sits centered or cocked to one side.

  2.  Check for any active warning lights — TPMS, VSA, EPS (electric power steering).

  3.  Observe steering effort when turning the wheel lock-to-lock slowly. Note any binding or uneven resistance side-to-side.

  4.  Shut engine off. Record your observations on the repair order before proceeding.

📚 Canvas Reference: Review the Alignment Angles Reference page — specifically the section on Caster and its effect on pull and returnability before proceeding to Step 2.

Step 2 — Pre-Alignment Inspection

A

Tire Condition & Pressure — All Four Corners

Check inflation pressure (cold) against door placard. Inspect tread depth and wear pattern. Mismatched pressure front-to-front is a common cause of pull. A low right front tire can mimic an alignment pull.

B

★ Verify Strut Installation — Right Front

Inspect the right front strut upper mount orientation. On a Honda Accord strut, the upper mount has a directional orientation mark — it must be positioned correctly or it will set the strut at an incorrect angle, directly causing pull and incorrect caster/camber.

Also verify: upper strut nut torqued to spec, lower strut pinch bolt torqued, and no binding in the strut bearing under steering input.

C

Inspect Ball Joints, Tie Rod Ends & Control Arm Bushings

With the vehicle on the rack, check for play in both front ball joints and both outer tie rod ends. Inspect lower control arm bushings for separation or incorrect seating. A loose or improperly reinstalled lower control arm after strut removal can cause pull by shifting the suspension geometry.

D

Measure Ride Height — Left Front vs. Right Front

Measure front ride height at both front corners per iN specification. A new OEM strut and spring should bring the right front to stock height. If the right front is significantly higher or lower than the left, the new spring or upper mount may be incorrectly installed or the wrong part was used.

📚 Canvas Reference: Refer to Suspension Component Inspection Notes for strut upper mount orientation procedure and torque specs. Review Alignment Angles Reference — Camber section, effect of strut angle on camber reading.

Step 4 — Root Cause Analysis

Based on the alignment data, the root cause of the pull is:

Primary cause — Incorrect strut upper mount installation: The right front upper strut mount was not indexed to the correct orientation during reassembly. On the Honda Accord, the upper mount has a positioning mark that must align with the reference mark on the strut tower. When installed off-orientation, the entire strut assembly is canted forward/outward — this simultaneously reduces caster (low positive caster = pull right) and increases positive camber on the right front.

Secondary cause — Front toe not reset: After strut replacement, the right front toe was not readjusted. Strut removal disturbs the wheel position, and toe must always be reset after any front strut or lower control arm service.

The SAI reading being within range confirms the knuckle, lower control arm, and subframe are not bent — the fault is in the strut installation only.

📚 Canvas Reference: Review the Vehicle-Specific Alignment Procedures page — note that alignment is always required after any front strut, lower control arm, or knuckle replacement. Confirm the Honda iN (Service Express) procedure for upper strut mount installation on the Accord for the correct orientation index mark location.

Step 5 — Recommended Fix & Repair Plan

1

Advise Customer — No Charge Correction

The pull is a direct result of the strut installation — the concern did not exist before the repair. Communicate this clearly to the service advisor. This correction should be performed at no charge to the customer as it was caused by the repair process.

2

Remove and Reinstall Right Front Strut Upper Mount — Correctly Oriented

Reference Honda iN for the upper mount orientation mark on the 2019 Accord. Remove the upper strut nut, index the mount to the correct position, and reinstall. Torque upper strut nut and lower strut pinch bolt to OEM spec.

3

Perform Full Four-Wheel Alignment

With the mount correctly installed, mount alignment heads and take new readings. Adjust front caster (if adjustable via aftermarket cam bolts or eccentric), front camber, front toe, and verify rear toe/thrust angle. Center the steering wheel. Print before and after alignment report — attach to repair order.

4

Verify Repair — Static Check

With the vehicle in park, engine running, confirm the steering wheel is centered, no pull felt with hands-off (on flat surface), and all alignment angles are within OEM spec on the printed report.

5

Document & Return to Customer

Complete the repair order with: root cause found, correction performed, alignment report attached, all torque specs documented. Reset TPMS if disturbed. Return vehicle.

Key Takeaway: Alignment is required after every front strut replacement, without exception. Upper mount orientation directly controls caster and camber — a missed indexing mark causes both angles to fail simultaneously.

🧠 Knowledge Check — Answer All 5 Questions

Question 1

The alignment data shows the right front caster is +1° 15' and the left front is +3° 00'. The OEM spec is +2° 30' ± 1° 00'. Which of the following best describes what is happening and why?

A.   Both sides are within spec; no action required

B.   Right caster is out of spec and the side-to-side difference exceeds the 0° 30' maximum; vehicle will pull right

C.   Left caster is out of spec; vehicle will pull left

D.   Caster does not cause pull; only toe causes pull

▶ Show Answer

✓ Correct Answer: B

Right caster at +1° 15' is below the minimum of +1° 30'. More critically, the side-to-side difference is 1° 45' — far exceeding the 0° 30' maximum allowable difference. The vehicle pulls toward the side with less positive caster — the right side. This is a direct result of the incorrectly oriented strut upper mount.

Question 2

The SAI readings are: Left 15° 10' / Right 15° 00'. What does this tell you about the suspension components on the right side?

A.   The lower control arm is bent and needs replacement

B.   The steering knuckle is damaged

C.   SAI is within normal range; the knuckle, lower control arm, and subframe are not damaged — the fault is in the strut/upper mount installation only

D.   SAI confirms the strut is bent

▶ Show Answer

✓ Correct Answer: C

SAI is a built-in, non-adjustable angle that reflects the geometry of the knuckle, lower control arm, and strut tower. Both sides read within the typical 14°–16° range and the side-to-side difference is only 0° 10' — well within the 1° tolerance. This rules out structural damage and confirms the alignment issue originates from the strut upper mount orientation, not a bent component.

Question 3

The right front toe reads 0° 28' toe-in. The OEM spec is 0° 10' ± 0° 10' (range: 0° to 0° 20'). Convert 0° 28' and 0° 20' into decimal degrees and explain what type of tire wear this condition will cause if left uncorrected.

▶ Show Answer

✓ Answer

Conversions: 0° 28' = 28 ÷ 60 = 0.47°  |  0° 20' = 20 ÷ 60 = 0.33°

The right front is 0.47° — exceeding the 0.33° maximum. Excess toe-in means the tire is pointed inward while rolling forward, causing the tread to scrub against the road surface laterally with every inch of travel. This produces feathered or saw-tooth diagonal wear across the tread blocks — the leading edges of the tread wear sharp while the trailing edges round off. This is the most rapid form of alignment-related tire wear.

Question 4

The thrust angle reads 0° 05'. The OEM spec is 0° ± 0° 10'. A classmate says "We should adjust the rear toe to fix the pull." Is this correct? Explain your reasoning.

▶ Show Answer

✓ Answer: No — this is incorrect.

The thrust angle of 0° 05' is within the 0° ± 0° 10' specification. The rear axle is tracking correctly and is not contributing to the pull. Adjusting the rear toe on a vehicle with a correct thrust angle would be unnecessary work and could introduce new problems. The pull in this case is 100% front-end in origin — caused by the unequal caster (strut mount orientation) and the subsequent front toe setting being disturbed. The classmate's suggestion would not address the root cause.

Question 5

As the technician, write a 3–5 sentence customer-friendly explanation of what caused the pull and what was done to fix it. Write it as if you are speaking to the service advisor who will relay it to the customer.

▶ Show Sample Answer

✓ Sample Answer

"When we replaced the right front strut, the upper mounting bracket — the part that connects the strut to the car body — was reinstalled slightly rotated from its correct position. This rotation changed the angle of the strut, which directly affected the wheel alignment on that corner, causing the car to pull to the right. We corrected the mount orientation to the proper position, then performed a full four-wheel alignment to bring all angles back within Honda's specifications. There is no charge for this correction since it was related to the repair we performed. The alignment printout is included with your paperwork."
