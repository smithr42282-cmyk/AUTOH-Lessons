Slide 2 of 6 — Bearing Fundamentals

Wheel Bearing Fundamentals

Modern Honda front wheel bearings are sealed, non-adjustable, press-fit hub unit assemblies. They support the vehicle's weight, allow smooth wheel rotation, and — critically — house the magnetic encoder ring (tone ring) that feeds wheel speed data to the ABS control unit.

Figure 1 — Honda sealed hub bearing unit. Encoder ring (red) is integral to the bearing — it cannot be serviced separately.

Functions of the Wheel Bearing Supports radial and axial loads · Allows smooth, low-friction wheel rotation · Houses the ABS encoder ring · Seals out water, road grit, and contaminants Failure Symptoms Rumbling/growling that changes with vehicle speed (not RPM) · Humming on curves (load-dependent) · Loose or rough feel on dial indicator check · Grinding or clicking at low speed

Bearing Inspection — Dial Indicator & Visual

📖 Honda iN / Service Express — Look Up First

Before measuring, retrieve the maximum allowable bearing play specification for your vehicle.

Service Information → [Year/Model] → Steering & Suspension → Hub/Bearing → Inspection → Bearing End Play Spec (mm)

Dial Indicator End-Play Test

  1. 1Raise and support vehicle. Allow front suspension to hang freely (unloaded). Chock rear wheels.
  2. 2Mount dial indicator magnetic base on a fixed point — brake caliper bracket or subframe. Never mount on a moving part.
  3. 3Place indicator probe on the hub face, perpendicular to the axle centerline for axial play measurement.
  4. 4Zero the dial indicator.
  5. 5Grasp the tire at 12 and 6 o'clock. Push/pull firmly. Record total indicator movement.
  6. 6Compare reading to Honda specification. Any play beyond spec = bearing replacement required.

Visual Inspection — When Disassembled

  1. 1Inspect knuckle bearing bore for scoring, corrosion, or oval wear that would prevent proper press-fit.
  2. 2Inspect hub splines and flange for cracks, corrosion, or damage.
  3. 3Inspect ABS wheel speed sensor and sensor mounting hole for damage before reinstall.
  4. 4Inspect the encoder ring on the NEW bearing for chips, cracks, or contamination before pressing in.

⚠ Critical — Sealed Bearing Units

Honda front wheel bearings are non-serviceable sealed units. If inspection reveals play beyond spec, or if the bearing is rough or noisy on spin check — replace the complete bearing unit. Do not pack or adjust sealed bearings.

Slide 4 of 6 — Magnetic Sensor Card

Magnetic Sensor Test Card — Pre-Install Verification

Before pressing a new sealed wheel bearing into the knuckle, the magnetic encoder ring must be verified with a magnetic sensor test card. The encoder ring is a multipole magnetic ring bonded to the bearing seal that the ABS wheel speed sensor reads. Installing a bearing with a damaged, reversed, or demagnetized encoder ring will cause an ABS fault immediately after install.

🧲

Magnetic Sensor Test Card

A thin, plastic-encased card containing a magnetized liquid crystal film. When held near the encoder ring, alternating north/south pole segments appear as a visible striped pattern. The card reveals polarity, consistency, and orientation of the magnetic encoder ring without any tools or power.

How to Use the Magnetic Sensor Test Card — Step by Step

  1. 1Obtain the new bearing before pressing. Handle only by outer race edges — avoid touching the encoder ring seal surface.
  2. 2Locate the encoder ring. It is the dark-colored magnetic seal ring on one face of the bearing (inboard side on most Honda applications). Confirm orientation against Honda service manual figure.
  3. 3Hold the test card flat against the encoder ring surface. The card face should make full, flush contact with the ring. Do not tilt at an angle — this produces inconsistent readings.
  4. 4Observe the striped pattern. A good encoder ring shows clear, evenly-spaced alternating dark and light stripes around the full circumference.
  5. 5Rotate the bearing slowly while maintaining card contact and confirm the stripe pattern is consistent with no missing, faded, or irregular segments.
  6. 6Check orientation. Some Honda applications specify which face of the bearing installs inboard. The encoder ring faces the ABS sensor — confirm the ring will face the sensor after installation.
  7. 7Document result on the job sheet before pressing bearing into knuckle.

✅ PASS — Proceed to Install

Clear, even, alternating stripes visible across full encoder ring circumference. Pattern consistent through full rotation.

❌ FAIL — Do Not Install

Missing segments, faded areas, no pattern visible, or irregular/uneven spacing. Return bearing — do not install a bearing that fails this check.

⚠ Why This Matters

A bearing with a damaged encoder ring will cause an ABS wheel speed sensor signal fault (DTC) the first time the vehicle moves. The test card check takes 30 seconds and prevents a return visit, repeat disassembly, and customer comebacks. It is not optional on Honda PACT work.

🧲 Keep the test card away from strong magnets, scan tools, and electronic components. Store flat in a protective sleeve. A demagnetized or bent test card gives false readings — inspect the card itself before using.

Slide 5 of 6 — Installation

Bearing Installation — After Sensor Card Verification

⚠ Do Not Proceed Without Completing Sensor Card Test

The magnetic sensor test card check (Slide 4) must be completed and passed before beginning any pressing operation. Record pass/fail on your job sheet.

Bearing Press-In Procedure — Honda Front Knuckle

  1. 1Clean the knuckle bore thoroughly. Remove all corrosion, old bearing material, and burrs. The bore must be clean and smooth. A rough bore prevents proper bearing seating and shortens bearing life.
  2. 2Apply a thin coat of clean brake fluid or assembly lubricant to the outside diameter of the new bearing and the inside of the knuckle bore. This aids press-in without damaging the bearing race.
  3. 3Start the bearing square in the knuckle bore by hand. Confirm encoder ring orientation — encoder ring faces inboard, toward ABS sensor location.
  4. 4Using the 62 × 68 mm attachment (07746-0010500) and driver (07749-0010000), press on the bearing outer race only. Never press on the inner race — this destroys the bearing.
  5. 5Press until bearing outer race is fully seated and flush with (or at specified depth below) the knuckle bore face. Confirm against Honda service spec.
  6. 6Install snap ring if applicable per Honda service manual for the model/year.
  7. 7Press hub into bearing inner race using hub dis/assembly tool 07GAF-SE00100. Hub flange face must be flush with inner race when fully seated.
  8. 8Torque spindle nut to Honda specification. Use a new spindle nut — Honda spindle nuts are single-use and must not be reused.
After Installation — Verify Hub rotates smoothly by hand with no roughness · Wheel speed sensor seated and torqued · No play on dial indicator check · Torque values recorded on job sheet Common Installation Mistakes Pressing on inner race instead of outer · Installing bearing backwards (encoder ring outboard) · Reusing spindle nut · Failing to clean bore before press-in

Spindle nut torque and bearing press-depth specs vary significantly by model year. Never use a generic spec.

Slide 6 of 6 — ABS Sensor & Post-Install

ABS Wheel Speed Sensor & Post-Install Verification

The ABS wheel speed sensor reads the magnetic encoder ring on the bearing. After bearing installation, the sensor must be properly installed and the system verified before returning the vehicle to service.

Wheel Speed Sensor Service

  1. 1Inspect sensor tip and mounting bore. Remove corrosion from the mounting hole with a wire brush. A corroded bore causes incorrect air gap and poor signal.
  2. 2Apply anti-seize to sensor threads and mounting bolts per Honda service manual direction.
  3. 3Seat sensor fully until it contacts the bearing outer race or sensor stop. Honda sensors are typically self-setting — no air gap adjustment required when the sensor seats fully.
  4. 4Torque sensor bolt to Honda specification. Overtorquing cracks the sensor body.
  5. 5Route and secure sensor harness in all factory clips. A misrouted harness can be damaged by suspension travel.

Post-Install Verification

  1. 1Check for DTCs with Honda diagnostic equipment before road test. Clear any installation-related codes.
  2. 2Perform a road test of at least 10 mph. Confirm ABS warning light extinguishes and no new DTCs set.
  3. 3Verify wheel speed data with scan tool data list — all four wheel speed sensor readings should be consistent and match vehicle speed.
  4. 4Confirm no bearing noise during road test — rumbling, growling, or humming after install indicates improper bearing seating.

⚠ If ABS Light Remains On After Install

First check: Was the magnetic sensor card test performed? Was the encoder ring facing the correct direction? Retrieve DTCs — a wheel speed sensor signal fault after bearing replacement almost always indicates an inverted or damaged encoder ring. Disassembly and bearing replacement will be required.

Question 1 of 5

What is the primary purpose of performing the magnetic sensor test card check on a new wheel bearing before installation?

✅ Correct! The magnetic sensor test card verifies encoder ring integrity, polarity, and orientation. An inverted or damaged encoder ring causes an ABS fault immediately after install. The 30-second check prevents a repeat disassembly.
❌ Not quite. The magnetic test card checks the encoder ring — the multipole magnetic ring on the bearing seal that the ABS wheel speed sensor reads. It confirms polarity, integrity, and proper orientation before the bearing is pressed in.
Question 2 of 5

When pressing a new wheel bearing into the Honda front knuckle, which surface must the press tool contact?

✅ Correct! Always press on the outer race when pressing a bearing into the knuckle. Pressing on the inner race loads the bearing balls and will immediately destroy the new bearing. Tool 07746-0010500 (62 × 68 mm attachment) is sized to contact the outer race.
❌ Not quite. Press on the outer race only when pressing the bearing into the knuckle. Pressing on the inner race transmits force through the rolling elements, which destroys the bearing. The 62 × 68 mm attachment (07746-0010500) is designed for this purpose.
Question 3 of 5

A new wheel bearing fails the magnetic sensor test card check — the card shows no visible stripe pattern on the encoder ring. What is the correct action?

✅ Correct! A bearing that fails the sensor card test must not be installed. There is no field repair for a damaged or demagnetized encoder ring. Return the part and obtain a replacement. Installing a failed bearing guarantees an ABS fault and a repeat job.
❌ Not quite. Return the bearing — do not install it. The encoder ring cannot be repaired in the field. An installed bearing with a failed encoder ring will set ABS wheel speed sensor DTCs and require complete disassembly again. The test card check exists precisely to prevent this.
Question 4 of 5

Which special tool is used to drive the hub in and out of the wheel bearing inner race without damaging the assembly?

✅ Correct! The hub dis/assembly tool 07GAF-SE00100 (40 mm) is the Honda-specific tool for pressing the hub in and out of the bearing inner race. Using generic tools or hammering the hub risks damage to the new bearing.
❌ Not quite. Tool 07GAF-SE00100 (Hub Dis/Assembly Tool, 40 mm) is the correct answer. The 62 × 68 mm attachment (07746-0010500) is for pressing the bearing outer race into the knuckle bore — a different operation.
Question 5 of 5

After completing wheel bearing replacement, the ABS warning light remains on during the road test. What is the most likely cause related to bearing installation?

✅ Correct! An ABS warning light after bearing replacement is nearly always caused by an inverted encoder ring or a damaged encoder ring that was installed without the magnetic test card check. The wheel speed sensor cannot read the encoder ring if it faces away from the sensor or is demagnetized.
❌ Not quite. An ABS light immediately after bearing replacement almost always points to an encoder ring issue — either installed backwards (encoder ring facing outboard instead of toward the sensor) or a damaged encoder ring that should have been caught by the magnetic test card check before install.
