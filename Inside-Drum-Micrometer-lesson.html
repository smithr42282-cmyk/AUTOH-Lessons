Slide 1 of 3 — Introduction

What Is an Inside Drum Micrometer?

An inside drum micrometer (also called a brake drum micrometer) is a precision measuring instrument used to determine the inside diameter of a brake drum. This reading tells you whether the drum is still within safe service limits.

🔧 Tool Design The two anvils extend outward to contact the drum's inner wall. Rotating the thimble moves the measuring anvils in or out until they make contact.
📋 ASE Standard NATEF/ASE Task V.C.1 — "Measure brake drum diameter; determine serviceability." Required at MLR, AST, and MAST levels.

⚠ Honda PACT Note

Always confirm the drum micrometer is zeroed and calibrated before measuring. A mis-calibrated tool is worse than no measurement at all.

Slide 2 of 3 — Reading the Measurement

How to Read an Inside Drum Micrometer

Inside drum micrometers use the same sleeve + thimble reading system as an outside micrometer — just applied to internal diameter.

Reading Steps:

  1. Sleeve reading — Count the last visible line on the barrel to the left of the thimble edge. Each major line = 1 mm (some tools use 0.025" increments in SAE).
  2. Thimble reading — Read the thimble line that lines up with the datum line on the sleeve. Each line = 0.01 mm.
  3. Add them together — Sleeve + Thimble = Total inside diameter.

229.0 mm0.15 mm229.15 mm
9.000"0.012"9.012"

⚠ Common Mistake

Students often forget to check if the thimble has passed a half-line on the sleeve, which adds an extra 0.5 mm. Always confirm both the main line AND the half-line before reading the thimble.

Slide 3 of 3 — Serviceability

Using Your Measurement: Is the Drum Serviceable?

A measurement alone means nothing without a specification to compare it to. Every drum has two critical numbers stamped directly on it:

📏 Maximum Diameter The largest allowable inside diameter after machining. If the drum has been turned or worn beyond this point, it must be replaced. Typically cast into the drum's hat or web.
🔄 Discard Diameter Some manufacturers list this separately as the dimension at which the drum is discarded even without machining. Always check Honda service data — the spec is vehicle-specific.
Example — Honda Civic Rear Drum:
Standard diameter: 180.0 mm  |  Maximum diameter: 181.0 mm
If your micrometer reads 181.3 mm → Drum is out of spec → Replace.

3-Step Decision Process:

  1. Take your micrometer reading.
  2. Look up the max diameter in the Honda Service Manual (or from the stamp on the drum).
  3. Compare: If measured < max → serviceable. If measured ≥ max → replace drum.

🏁 Honda PACT Application

When writing your repair order, record the actual measurement, the spec, and your determination. "Drum measures 181.3 mm; spec is 181.0 mm max; recommend replacement" is the Three C format — Concern, Cause, Correction.

Practice Measurement Questions

5 Practice Questions — Inside Drum Micrometer

Question 1 of 5

A drum micrometer sleeve reads 228.0 mm and the thimble reads 0.45 mm. What is the inside drum diameter?

✅ Correct! 228.0 + 0.45 = 228.45 mm. You simply add the sleeve and thimble readings together.
❌ Not quite. Add sleeve + thimble: 228.0 mm + 0.45 mm = 228.45 mm (Choice B). Watch decimal placement.
Question 2 of 5

The Honda service manual lists the maximum brake drum diameter as 181.0 mm. Your micrometer reads 180.72 mm. What is the correct determination?

✅ Correct! 180.72 mm is less than the 181.0 mm max → drum is still within specification and serviceable.
❌ Not quite. 180.72 mm is less than 181.0 mm max, meaning the drum is still within spec and serviceable (Choice B). Only replace if measured ≥ max.
Question 3 of 5

A sleeve reads 9.000" and the thimble reads 0.018". A second measurement at 90° reads 9.021". What condition does this indicate?

✅ Correct! Two different readings taken 90° apart (9.018" vs 9.021") indicate the drum is out-of-round. This causes brake pulsation and pedal feedback during stops.
❌ Not quite. When two diameters measured 90° apart are different (9.018" vs 9.021"), the drum is out-of-round (Choice C). Taper would be different readings along the drum's depth, not across it.
Question 4 of 5

Before measuring a brake drum, what is the first step a technician should always perform with the drum micrometer?

✅ Correct! Always zero and verify calibration before use. A mis-calibrated micrometer gives a false reading, which could pass a drum that should be replaced — a safety issue.
❌ Not quite. The first step is always to verify calibration and zero the tool (Choice C). While cleaning the drum is good practice, an uncalibrated tool is the bigger safety risk.
Question 5 of 5

A drum reads 229.0 mm on the sleeve. The thimble line aligns at 0.5 on the scale, but you can also see that the sleeve's half-line is visible. What is the correct total measurement?

✅ Correct! Sleeve (229.0) + Half-line (0.5) + Thimble (0.5) = 230.00 mm. Remember: when the half-line is visible, add 0.5 mm before adding the thimble reading.
❌ Not quite. The half-line visible on the sleeve adds 0.5 mm: 229.0 + 0.5 (half-line) + 0.5 (thimble) = 230.00 mm (Choice D). This is the most common reading error on exams!
